How to use this app
Upon entering the page, you'll encounter an engaging maze! You have the option to tackle the challenge on your own or opt for an automated solution by clicking the 'Solve' button, demonstrating how a breadth-first search algorithm navigates through the puzzle.

If you wish to customize the maze, utilize the four buttons beneath the puzzle labeled: Draw, Erase, Start, and End. These buttons allow you to place tiles strategically.

Keep in mind that you can only set one starting point and one ending point; repositioning them will replace the previous ones. Once satisfied with your maze configuration, use the 'Clear' button to wipe the slate clean and remove all tiles from the maze!

What is Breadth First Search?
Breadth-first search (BFS) is an algorithm that traverses a graph in waves. Imagine dropping water into a maze, BFS explores everything simultaneously!

This characteristic makes BFS particularly advantageous in scenarios where the end-goal might be too obstructed for a depth-first search to uncover. For instance, navigating an open field would render a depth-first search less effective.

Advanced pathfinding algorithms, like A-Star, draw inspiration from the broad exploration of BFS waves. In the realm of video games, A-Star is commonly employed for enemy AI to detect the player's location.

BFS is not only limited to mazes but finds applications in network routing, social network analysis, and more. Its versatility makes it a fundamental algorithm in computer science.
